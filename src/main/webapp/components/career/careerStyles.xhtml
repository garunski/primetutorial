Work Styles

Included in the list below are the personal work style characteristics that can affect how well a worker is likely to perform in this occupation.

Personal Characteristics

  • Cooperation - Being pleasant with others and displaying a good-natured, cooperative attitude
  • Self-Control - Maintaining composure, keeping emotions in check, controlling anger, and avoiding aggressive behavior
  • Stress Tolerance - Accepting criticism and dealing calmly with high-stress situations
  • Adaptability/Flexibility - Being open to change and to considerable variety in the workplace
  • Dependability - Being reliable, responsible, and dependable, and fulfilling obligations

Work Approach

  • Persistence - Persistence in the face of obstacles
  • Initiative - Willingness to take on responsibilities and challenges
  • Leadership - Willingness to lead, take charge, and offer opinions and direction
  • Achievement/Effort - Establishing and maintaining personally challenging goals and exerting effort toward mastering tasks
  • Innovation - Creativity and alternative thinking to develop new ideas and answers to work-related problems

* Work Styles are measured by how important the style is to this occupation, where one is 'not important' and five is 'very important'.
